Understanding Metadata Risks in Period Tracking

What Is Metadata in Health Apps?

Metadata is data about data. In the context of your cycle data, this includes timestamps, location, and device information. While metadata may not reveal sensitive health details directly, it can expose users to targeted advertising or data leaks.

  • Examples of metadata:
    • Time of last menstrual cycle (TLC)
    • Location history
    • Device identifiers
  • Privacy discussions often overlook metadata risks.

Apps collect cycle data to provide insights, but bad actors can exploit this if security is lax. Location data, for instance, can reveal a user's daily routine, potentially leading to identity theft or invasive targeted advertising.
